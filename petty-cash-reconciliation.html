Petty Cash Reconciliation: Complete Procedure Guide

Learn how to reconcile petty cash properly, resolve discrepancies, and maintain strong internal controls for your office fund.

Automate Reconciliation Read Guide

What is Petty Cash Reconciliation?

Petty cash reconciliation is the process of verifying that your physical cash on hand matches your petty cash log records. It's a critical internal control that ensures accountability, detects errors or fraud, and maintains accurate financial records for your office.

Internal Controls

Regular reconciliation prevents unauthorized withdrawals and ensures proper oversight of office funds.

Error Detection

Catch calculation mistakes, missing receipts, and recording errors before they become larger problems.

Audit Compliance

Maintain proper documentation and audit trail for internal and external financial reviews.

How to Reconcile Petty Cash: Step-by-Step Procedure

Follow this standard petty cash reconciliation procedure to ensure accuracy and maintain internal controls:

1

Count Physical Cash

Count all bills and coins in your petty cash box, drawer, or envelope. Record the exact amount. For best practices, have a second person witness the count.

Example: Physical cash count = $127.50
2

Total All Receipts and Vouchers

Add up all receipts, vouchers, and expense documentation since the last reconciliation. Verify each receipt is properly signed and documented in your petty cash log.

Example: Total receipts = $72.50
3

Calculate Expected Balance

Use this formula: Starting Balance - Total Receipts = Expected Cash

Example: $200.00 (starting) - $72.50 (receipts) = $127.50 (expected)
4

Compare and Identify Discrepancies

Compare your physical cash count to the expected balance. If they match, your petty cash is reconciled. If not, you have a discrepancy that needs investigation.

Example: $127.50 (physical) = $127.50 (expected) ✓ Reconciled!
5

Document the Reconciliation

Record the reconciliation date, physical cash count, total receipts, and any discrepancies. Both the petty cash custodian and reviewer should sign the reconciliation report.

6

Replenish the Fund (If Needed)

If your petty cash is running low, submit receipts to accounting for reimbursement and restore the fund to its original balance (e.g., $200).

Common Petty Cash Discrepancies and How to Resolve Them

Missing Receipts

Cause: Employee forgot to submit receipt or lost it.

Solution: Require receipts within 24 hours. For lost receipts, have employee sign a petty cash voucher explaining the expense.

Calculation Errors

Cause: Math mistakes in manual petty cash log or running balance.

Solution: Double-check all calculations. Use digital tracking software with automatic calculations to eliminate errors.

Incorrect Change

Cause: Wrong amount given when disbursing cash.

Solution: Count cash twice when disbursing. Have recipient verify and sign for the exact amount received.

Duplicate Entries

Cause: Same transaction recorded twice in the log.

Solution: Review log entries carefully. Use sequential voucher numbers to track each transaction uniquely.

Unauthorized Withdrawals

Cause: Cash taken without proper documentation or approval.

Solution: Limit access to petty cash. Require dual signatures for amounts over a threshold (e.g., $25).

Unexplained Shortages

Cause: Unknown - could be theft, lost cash, or unrecorded expense.

Solution: Document as "cash short" and investigate. Implement stronger controls like locked cash box and limited access.

Internal Controls for Petty Cash: Best Practices

Strong internal controls prevent fraud, reduce errors, and ensure your petty cash reconciliation runs smoothly:

Separation of Duties

The person who maintains the petty cash log should not be the same person who reconciles it. This prevents fraud and ensures independent verification.

Set Maximum Limits

Establish a maximum fund size (e.g., $300) and per-transaction limit (e.g., $50). Larger expenses should go through formal purchasing procedures.

Require Documentation

Every disbursement needs a signed petty cash voucher and original receipt. No exceptions, even for small amounts.

Secure Storage

Keep petty cash in a locked box or drawer. Limit access to authorized personnel only (typically 1-2 people).

Regular Reconciliation

Reconcile at least monthly, or weekly for high-volume offices. Surprise reconciliations also help deter fraud.

Approval Process

Require manager approval for petty cash disbursements over a certain amount or for specific expense categories.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should petty cash be reconciled?

Most organizations reconcile petty cash monthly, but high-volume offices should reconcile weekly. The frequency depends on transaction volume and internal control requirements. More frequent reconciliation helps catch errors and discrepancies early.

What causes petty cash discrepancies?

Common causes include: missing receipts, calculation errors in the log, unauthorized withdrawals, incorrect change given, or receipts recorded at wrong amounts. Strong internal controls and regular reconciliation minimize these issues.

Who should perform petty cash reconciliation?

Best practice is to have someone other than the petty cash custodian perform reconciliation. This separation of duties is a key internal control that prevents fraud and ensures accountability.

What should I do if petty cash doesn't reconcile?

First, recount the cash and re-verify all receipts. Check for math errors in your log. Review recent transactions for missing or duplicate entries. If the discrepancy persists, document it formally and investigate potential causes. Small discrepancies (under $5) may be recorded as "cash over/short", but larger amounts require thorough investigation.

What is a petty cash reconciliation report?

A petty cash reconciliation report documents: the reconciliation date, starting balance, total disbursements, physical cash count, expected vs. actual balance, any discrepancies, and signatures from both the custodian and reviewer. This report provides an audit trail for your petty cash fund.

Can I reconcile petty cash without receipts?

No. Proper petty cash reconciliation requires receipts for every disbursement. Without receipts, you cannot verify expenses or maintain an audit trail. If a receipt is lost, the employee should complete a petty cash voucher explaining the expense and have it approved by a manager.
